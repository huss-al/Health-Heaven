Healthy Tikka Masala

Enjoy the spicy, rich flavours of tikka masala with this family-friendly lighter version that's both healthy and gluten-free. It's a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Enjoy the spicy, rich flavours of tikka masala with this family-friendly lighter version that's both healthy and gluten-free. It's a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Cooking time:

  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 large garlic cloves
  • thumb-sized piece of ginger
  • 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • 4 small skinless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp tikka spice powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 40g ground almonds
  • 200g spinach
  • 3 tbsp fat-free natural yogurt
  • ½ small bunch of coriander, chopped
  • brown basmati rice, to serve
Instructions
  1. Put the onion, garlic and ginger in a food processor and whizz to a smooth paste.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil in a flameproof casserole dish over a medium heat.
  3. Add the onion mixture and fry for 15 mins.
  4. Tip into a bowl and wipe out the pan.
  5. Add the remaining oil and the chicken and fry for 5-7 mins, or until lightly brown.
  6. Stir in the tikka spice and cayenne and fry for a further minute.
  7. Tip the onion mixture back into the pan, along with the tomatoes and 1 can full of water.
  8. Bring to the boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook, uncovered, for 15 mins.
  9. Stir in the almonds and spinach and cook for a further 10 mins. Season, then stir through the yogurt and coriander.
  10. Serve with brown rice.

Healthy Bolognese

This low-fat, low-calorie bolognese combines lean pork mince with fennel and cherry tomatoes to give a burst of fresh flavours and two of your five-a-day.

This low-fat, low-calorie bolognese combines lean pork mince with fennel and cherry tomatoes to give a burst of fresh flavours and two of your five-a-day.

Prep time:

Cooking time:

Total time:

Servings:

5 mins

25 mins

35 mins

2

Ingredients
  • 100g wholewheat linguine
  • 2 tsp rapeseed oil
  • 1 fennel bulb, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 200g pork mince with less than 5% fat
  • 200g whole cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp vegetable bouillon powder
  • generous handful chopped basil
  • 100g wholewheat linguine
  • 2 tsp rapeseed oil
  • 1 fennel bulb, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 200g pork mince with less than 5% fat
  • 200g whole cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp vegetable bouillon powder
  • generous handful chopped basil
Instructions
  1. Bring a large pan of water to the boil, then cook the linguine following pack instructions, about 10 mins.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a non-stick wok or wide pan.
  3. Add the fennel and garlic and cook, stirring every now and then, until tender, about 10 mins.
  4. Tip in the pork and stir-fry until it changes colour, breaking it up as you go so there are no large clumps.
  5. Add the tomatoes, vinegar and bouillon, then cover the pan and cook for 10 mins over a low heat until the tomatoes burst and the pork is cooked and tender.
  6. Add the linguine and basil and plenty of pepper, and toss well before serving.

  12. Add the linguine and basil and plenty of pepper, and toss well before serving.

Healthy Roast Dinner

Cook a healthy, low-fat version of roast beef for two, made with lean, juicy steak and roasted veg, plus gravy on the side.

Cook a healthy, low-fat version of roast beef for two, made with lean, juicy steak and roasted veg, plus gravy on the side.

Prep time:

Cooking time:

Total time:

Servings:

15 mins

50 mins

65 mins

2

Ingredients
  • 285g medium potatoes, thickly sliced
  • 4 small carrots (160g), halved lengthways
  • 2 x 80g red onions, cut into quarters
  • 170g large Brussels sprouts (about 8-10), trimmed
  • 2½ tsp rapeseed oil
  • 2 tsp thyme leaves
  • 2 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 large garlic clove, finely grated
  • 2 pinches of English mustard powder
  • 170g thick, lean fillet steak
  • ½ tsp vegetable bouillon powder
  • 285g medium potatoes, thickly sliced
  • 4 small carrots (160g), halved lengthways
  • 2 x 80g red onions, cut into quarters
  • 170g large Brussels sprouts (about 8-10), trimmed
  • 2½ tsp rapeseed oil
  • 2 tsp thyme leaves
  • 2 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 large garlic clove, finely grated
  • 2 pinches of English mustard powder
  • 170g thick, lean fillet steak
  • ½ tsp vegetable bouillon powder
Instructions
  1. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Bring a large pan of water to the boil and cook the potatoes for 5 mins.
  3. Drain, reserving the water.
  4. Toss the potatoes, carrots, onions and sprouts with 2 tsp of the oil to coat.
  5. Arrange on a non-stick baking sheet, spaced apart.
  6. Scatter with 1 tsp of the thyme, grind over some black pepper, then roast for 30 mins.
  7. Meanwhile, mix 1 tsp of the vinegar with the garlic, remaining thyme and oil, the mustard and plenty of black pepper.
  8. Rub this over the steak, put in a shallow dish and set aside.
  9. Mix the rest of the vinegar with the bouillon and 125ml of the reserved water from step 1, then set aside.
  10. After 30 mins, turn the veg over and roast for 15 mins more.
  11. Meanwhile, heat a small non-stick frying pan over a medium-high heat.
  12. Lift the steak out of the marinade, shake off the excess and fry for 2-3 mins on each side until cooked to your liking.
  13. Remove to a board and leave to rest.
  14. Pour the leftover marinade into the frying pan and bubble until thickened slightly to make a gravy.
  15. Slice the steak and serve with the roast veg and gravy on the side.
