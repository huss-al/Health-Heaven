Versatile Veg Soup

Try a basic soup recipe that can be adapted for whatever veg you've got in the fridge. Serve with creme fraiche and fresh herbs.

Prep time:
Cooking time:
Total time:
Servings:

10 mins

15 mins

25 mins

2

Ingredients
  • 200g chopped vegetables such as onions, celery and carrots
  • 300g potatoes, cubed
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 700ml stock
  • crème fraîche and fresh herbs, to serve
Instructions
  1. Fry the vegetables and potatoes in a pan with the oil for a few minutes until beginning to soften.
  2. Cover with the stock and simmer for 10-15 mins until the veg is tender.
  3. Blend until smooth, then season.
  4. Serve with a dollop of crème fraîche and some fresh herbs. (Will freeze for up to one month.)

Roasted Cauliflower Steaks

Unlock the flavours of cauliflower with a red pepper, olive and caper salsa, topped with almonds. Healthy and vegan, it makes a tasty light lunch or supper.

Prep time:
Cooking time:
Total time:
Servings:

15 mins

15 mins

30 mins

2

Ingredients
  • 1 cauliflower
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 roasted red pepper
  • 4 black olives, pitted
  • small handful parsley
  • 1 tsp capers
  • ½ tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp toasted flaked almonds
  • 1 cauliflower
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 roasted red pepper
  • 4 black olives, pitted
  • small handful parsley
  • 1 tsp capers
  • ½ tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp toasted flaked almonds
Instructions
  1. Heat oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7 and line a baking tray with baking parchment.
  2. Slice the cauliflower into two 1-inch steaks – use the middle part as it’s larger, and save the rest for another time.
  3. Rub the paprika and ½ tbsp oil over the steaks and season.
  4. Put on the tray and roast for 15-20 mins until cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, make the salsa. Chop the pepper, olives, parsley and capers, and put into a bowl and mix with the remaining oil and vinegar.
  6. Season to taste.
  7. When the steaks are cooked, spoon over the salsa and top with flaked almonds to serve.

VSpicy Chicken & Avocado Wraps

Pan-fry lean chicken breast with lime, chilli and garlic, then pile onto seeded tortilla wraps. Cool before assembling if packing for lunch.

Cooking time:
Total time:
Servings:

5 mins

13 mins

18 mins

Ingredients
  • 1 chicken breast (approx 180g), thinly sliced at an angle
  • generous squeeze juice 0.5 lime
  • ½ tsp mild chilli powder
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 2 seeded wraps
  • 1 avocado, halved and stoned
  • 1 roasted red pepper from a jar, sliced
  • a few sprigs coriander, chopped
  • 1 chicken breast (approx 180g), thinly sliced at an angle
  • generous squeeze juice 0.5 lime
  • ½ tsp mild chilli powder
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 2 seeded wraps
  • 1 avocado, halved and stoned
  • 1 roasted red pepper from a jar, sliced
  • a few sprigs coriander, chopped
Instructions
  1. Mix the chicken with the lime juice, chilli powder and garlic.
  2. Heat the oil in a non-stick frying pan then fry the chicken for a couple of mins – it will cook very quickly so keep an eye on it.
  3. Meanwhile, warm the wraps following the pack instructions or, if you have a gas hob, heat them over the flame to slightly char them.
  4. Do not let them dry out or they are difficult to roll.
  5. Squash half an avocado onto each wrap, add the peppers to the pan to warm them through then pile onto the wraps with the chicken, and sprinkle over the coriander.
  6. Roll up, cut in half and eat with your fingers.
